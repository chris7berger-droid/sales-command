FINDINGS (each verified — code read + QB queried directly per [[qb-reconcile-query-qb-directly]])
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
(i) POPULATION IS 67, NOT 40. `status='Sent' AND qb_invoice_id IS NOT NULL` = 67. B62's row says
    40. The 15-invoice sample is 15-of-67 (~22%) — the unmeasured tail is bigger than filed.

(ii) GROUND TRUTH IS NOT "QB KNOWS, SC DOESN'T." This is the single most important finding and it
    inverts B62's premise for some invoices. DA Builders #10004: SC says **Paid** (correct — the
    customer did pay), but QB invoice 146928 reads **balance $29,671.17 OPEN**, with a matching
    $29,671.16 payment (ref 3108, 2026-06-02) sitting **UNAPPLIED**. A sync reading QB naively
    would flip a correct Paid back to unpaid.
    => DESIGN RULE: QB is only truth once payments are APPLIED to invoices. An unapplied payment
       makes a paid invoice look unpaid. Any sync must detect this case, not act on it.

(iii) $66,529 SITS UNAPPLIED IN QB (A/R Aging Detail, 2026-07-14):
       - Complete Contracting / Boot Barn  $35,296.39  (ref 012032, 2026-06-23)  -> see B-NEW-1
       - DA Builders                       $29,671.16  (ref 3108,   2026-06-02)
       - DA Builders                       $1,561.65   (ref 3162,   2026-07-02)
     NOT SC-caused. A QB-side application gap. Chris confirmed DA Builders' two ~$29,671 payments
     are legitimate (~$60k job paid in two halves) — NOT duplicates. WITHDRAWN: my earlier claim
     that these were SC-created duplicates.

(iv) EVERY SC "Paid" IS MANUAL (Chris, 2026-07-14). No automatic path exists. SC has pushed a QB
     payment only 7 times EVER (of 91 invoices). So QB is where cash actually lands, ~always, and
     SC's payment model is effectively write-only. The corruption loop is human-triggered and
     stops the moment Mark-as-Paid is paused. WITHDRAWN: my earlier "this is getting worse on its
     own every week" — it only moves when someone clicks the button.

(v) `qb-record-payment` NEVER SUBTRACTS RETENTION. `netAmount = amount - discount`
    (qb-record-payment/index.ts:154), but qb-sync-invoice:290 puts retention on the QB invoice as
    a NEGATIVE line, so QB TotalAmt is already net. Retention shipped 2026-04-20 (e925181) to the
    invoice push; the payment push has NEVER had a retention commit (checked every commit on that
    file). Real instance: #10031 pushed $31,232.80 against a $29,671.16 QB invoice. Only ever
    exercised twice (#10031, #10104) — small blast radius. Sequence with ADJ-1 in block 5.

(vi) RETENTION WAS NEVER BROKEN ON THE DETAIL PAGE. WITHDRAWN: my claim that "retention money is
     hidden." The Bill Retention button (Invoices.jsx:2581) already gates on `retention_released`
     and is status-independent — retention was never unbillable. ONLY the roll-up list/StatCards
     were wrong. See CHANGES SHIPPED.

(vii) ON "WHAT COUNTS AS PAID" (B62 design question (a)). B62's hypothesis was "summed linked QB
     Payments vs. what SC expects to collect." Recommendation: drop the SC side of that
     comparison — SC's expectation goes stale the moment a bookkeeper edits the QB invoice. Ask
     QuickBooks both questions instead:
       - TotalAmt minus applied payments == 0  -> cash actually arrived  -> genuinely paid
       - Balance == 0 but cash-owed > 0        -> credit memo / write-off / JE -> NOT paid, report
       - Balance > 0 with payments > 0         -> partial (SC has no state for this)
     Two further traps: sum the PER-INVOICE LinkedTxn allocation, never Payment.TotalAmt (one
     check paying three invoices would triple-count); and payments are MUTABLE (a bookkeeper can
     unapply a check), so "paid" is a comparison that can run backwards, not an event to latch.
     Blocks (b)-(e) of B62 were NOT worked — session ended first.
